Ray Kassar

President and CEO Atari. Hired in 1978 by Warner to transform Atari from startup into Corporation.

3 Dimensions
East Coast Business man in a three piece suit. Professional. 
Expert Marketer.
Thinks he saved the company from itself. Deluded.
Has no interest or knowledge of video games or industry. Clueless. Won't take big risks.

Goals
Transform Atari. Leverage existing assets and technologies.

Barriers
Engineers, Nolan Bushnell, Competition (especially Coleco), his own ignorance.

Arc
From hero to zero. In 1982 he was the man who made Atari a billion dollar corporation. In 1983 he was the villain who destroyed the company.

Nolan Bushnell

Founder of Atari in 1972. Fired or quit in 1978. Founded Catalyst and Chucky Cheese.

3 Dimensions
Entrepreneur. Knack for seeing the next big thing.
Fun California guy. Doesn't take himself or his job too seriously. Bored by business.
Doesn't respond to authority. Can't share power.
Gets more pleasure when everything is risked.

Goals
Have a good time making things people love. Create new things.

Barriers
Money and market factors, Management, Ray Kassar.

Arc
Has fun at Atari until business gets serious. Makes Mephistophelian deal with Warner. Becomes disillusioned and frustrated. Refuses to "retire to the beach" and founds several more companies.

Manny Gerrard

VP of Warner Communications. Manages the purchase and eventual sale of Atari.

3 Dimensions
East Coast Business man.
Chooses the wrong side in battles. Stuck in middle of fights between NB & RK, SR & RK
Disconnected from day-to-day operations.

Goals
Bring Warner back to profitability. Reorganize company at all costs.

Barriers
Engineers and lack of video game knowledge. Management that has high turnover. Ray Kassar, eventually.

Arc
Initially proposes buyout of Atari. Supports Kassar against Nolan Bushnell.

Steve Ross

CEO of Warner Communications. Manages the purchase and eventual sale of Atari.

3 Dimensions
Bumps elbows with celebrities. Considered father figure by Spielberg and others.
Spots future of communications business. Visionary, but doesn't understand games.
Exercises veto power at the wrong times.

Goals
Bring Warner back to profitability. Transform Warner from faltering small company into major media conglomerate.

Barriers

Arc
Makes fatal business decisions that would be blamed on Kassar: ex. licensing ET. He doesn't lose sight of bigger media picture. Invests heavily in cable TV eventually merges company with Time.

Jack Tramiel

Founder an CEO of Commodore

3 Dimensions
Holocaust survivor. Rags to riches.
Despot. Very hard to work for or work with.
Holds grudges for many, many years. Extremely competitive.
Will bankrupt his own suppliers by not paying bills and then buy them out.

Goals
Destroy the competition, conquer the world.

Barriers
Texas Instruments, Atari, Commodore, his own arrogance and attitude.

Arc
He is a villain bent on destroying his competition at TI and Atari. Gets fired from his own company buys Atari and tries to destroy Commodore.

Coleco

Arnold Greenberg: CEO. Al Kahn VP licensing

3 Dimensions
Unfocused. Expands into computers and eventually toys.
Underdog, with devious plans. Willing to trick Atari into over paying for licenses.
Good at spotting fads: Donkey Kong, Cabbage Patch Kids.
Has strong relationships with coin-op developers that it leverages.

Goals
Wants to replace Atari as biggest entertainment/game company. Also wants to do computers. Also wants to do toys.

Barriers
Much smaller than Atari.

Arc
Never surpass Atari in the public's eye, but their tough competition helps bring Atari and themselves down. Started as high-quality manufacturers, eventually forced


Alan Miller, David Crane, Bob Whitehead, Larry Kaplan

Engineers at Atari. Quit to form Activision

3 Dimensions
Extremely talent engineers
Very disgruntled. Need recognition in terms of both money and credit
Entrepreneurial, willing to take risks.

Goals
Get recognition and just pay for their efforts

Barriers
Ray Kassar, lack of recognition. Other third party developers

Arc
Oops. Who left the door open? They leave Atari to form their own high quality software company setting the model for companies that would bring the whole industry down.

Howard Scott Warshaw

Best and worst designer at Atari. Responsible for Yar's Revenge and ET

3 Dimensions
Has a sense of humor (Yar is Ray backwards. Alien bad guy in game is Rassak – Kassar backwards)
Talented designer. Able to turn out Raiders & ET in six weeks.
Spielberg's chosen designer.

Goals
Make fun games. Push limits of Atari as a system, at first. Then just finish under deadline.

Barriers
Time and pressure of huge licenses

Arc
Fallen angel or buffoon? Makes one of the best games for Atari and one of the worst. Has a sense of humor about it. Became a psychologist.
